Dr. René Favaloro

The man who saved thousands of lives

René Favaloro's life

  • 1923 - René Gerónimo Favaloro is born on July 12 in La Plata, Argentina.
  • 1949: Graduates as a doctor from the National University of La Plata.
  • 1950 - Begins his medical career in a small town in La Pampa, Jacinto Aráuz, where he improves community health and establishes the region’s first blood bank.
  • 1962 - Moves to the United States to specialize in thoracic and cardiovascular surgery at the Cleveland Clinic.
  • 1967 - Successfully performs the first coronary artery bypass surgery in the world, a milestone that revolutionizes the treatment of coronary diseases.
  • 1971 - Returns to Argentina with the vision of improving medicine in his country and sharing the knowledge acquired abroad.
  • 1975 - Founds the Favaloro Foundation in Buenos Aires, dedicated to medical research and education in Argentina.
  • 1992 - Establishes the Institute of Cardiology and Cardiovascular Surgery, part of the Favaloro Foundation, to provide high-quality medical care in Argentina.
  • 2000 - Passes away on July 29 in Buenos Aires. His death highlights the financial challenges faced by the Foundation and has a profound impact in Argentina and the international medical community.
